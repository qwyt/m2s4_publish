We can see that the structure of the survey has not been consistent over the years. Only a few questions were included in every survey. Most questions asked in 2014 and some in 2016 were not repeated in future surveys. This complicates the analysis because not all answers between different years can be compared and the amount of data which can be analyzed is lower.

However between 2017-2019 all the questions included in surveys were the same.

Looking at the summary of some of the main questions which were included in the survey every year we can see that:

  • Companies where males are overrepresented seem to provide mental health coverage less often.
  • Relatively few companies outside the US and Canada seem to provide metal health coverage (answer might not be comparable due to differences in healthcare systems and insurance models)
  • Americans are more likely to believe that their anonymity is protected when responding to the survey.
  • Workers in US/CAN are likely to have sought treatment but are less likely to discuss this in interviews.
